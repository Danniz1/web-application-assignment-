What is the Internet?

The Internet is a global network of billions of computers and electronic devices. It allows you to access information and communicate with anyone in the world.

It started in 1969 as a US military experiment called ARPANET and later expanded to universities, research centers, and finally the public.

Key facts:

  • Uses standard protocols like TCP/IP to communicate
  • Connects devices worldwide - computers, phones, servers
  • Supports services like email, web browsing, file sharing, and streaming
  • No single organization owns the Internet

Related terms: TCP/IP, World Wide Web, DNS
